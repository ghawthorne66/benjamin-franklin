Signs of a Pipe or Sewer Problem

Because most of your home’s pipes and sewer lines are located in the walls or below the ground, it’s important that you are aware of the signs of a problem. Knowing what to look for can help you identify minor issues before they have a chance to develop into major problems.

Common signs of pipe and sewer problems, such as breaks or leaks, include:

  • Unexplained increases on your monthly water bill
  • Foul, unpleasant odors or the smell of sewage
  • Damp or soggy patches in your yard/landscaping
  • Water damage on your floors, walls, or ceiling
  • Mold or mildew growth in or around your home
  • Reduced water pressure from shower heads/faucets
  • Lush patches in your lawn, despite even watering

If you notice any of the above signs or other symptoms of a pipe or sewer line problem, contact the plumbing professionals at Benjamin Franklin Plumbing® right away. There are a wide number of potential causes for your pipe problem, and our team has the high-tech equipment and cutting-edge tools to pinpoint the problem and determine its cause.
